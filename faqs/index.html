### Why Bitcoin? When referring to the network and its consensus rules (including the implied monetary system) the term "Bitcoin" is used, as opposed to "bitcoin", which is used to describe the underlying asset. Since its inception more than a decade ago, the network has proved to be resilient, all while successfully implementing a monetary policy with a known, verifiable and unalterable supply schedule. This makes bitcoin, the underlying asset, unique in its properties in being free from risks associated with central bank issued currencies (debasement and inflation) and any other assets which, in one way or another are compromised by geopolitical, or sector specific economic risk. ### What are the best practices when using bitcoin?
